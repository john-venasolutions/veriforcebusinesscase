Problem statement

We do not have a scalable FP&A process today.

NetSuite is becoming the actuals system and Fabric is becoming the data backbone, but budgeting, forecasting, management reporting, and board support still sit in large workbooks, manual adjustments, and one-off logic. The result is a finance process that works, but takes too much effort to maintain, is harder to trust than it should be, and does not give leadership answers as quickly as the business needs.

Why now

We are already redesigning the process.

NetSuite rollout is still moving, acquisitions continue to add new entities and data structures, and Budget 27 design work is starting now. If this is not addressed in time, the likely outcome is another planning cycle built on the same manual process. If it is addressed now, reporting and forecast support can improve first, and that work can roll directly into a cleaner budget process.

1What is getting in the way today
This is not an Excel problem by itself. It is a process, ownership, and scale problem.

Current friction

  • Budgeting and forecasting are still handled in Excel while NetSuite is mostly actuals.
  • Management reporting still depends on large workbooks, manual refreshes, and custom logic.
  • Some subsidiaries still need to provide manual inputs because not everything is fully standardized yet.
  • Intercompany activity, multiple entities, multiple currencies, and acquisitions add more moving parts every cycle.

Impact on finance

  • Too much time goes into building, fixing, and reconciling reports instead of analyzing what changed.
  • Ad hoc requests can still take days when leadership needs answers quickly.
  • Confidence in the numbers depends too much on who built the file and how recently it was updated.
  • Finance is still handholding the process more than running a repeatable system.

What good looks like

  • One refresh into one model instead of multiple downloads, exports, and manual uploads.
  • One reporting structure that can switch by region, entity, and currency without rebuilding files.
  • Faster drilldown from a summary view into the detail behind the number.
  • A process the team can run every month, quarter, and budget cycle without reinventing it.

6How the main options compare
The goal is not to pick the platform with the longest feature list. The goal is to pick the platform that best fits what finance needs to fix first.

The goal is not to pick the platform with the longest feature list. The goal is to pick the platform that best fits the immediate success criteria: faster management reporting, cleaner forecast support, easier adoption, lower maintenance burden, stronger finance ownership, and room to expand as NetSuite, Fabric, and broader data standardization continue.

Cross-functional planning

Anaplan

Best known for large planning programs that span finance, supply chain, sales, and workforce.

Where it is strong

  • Broad enterprise planning across multiple functions
  • Complex scenario modeling at scale
  • Organizations that want to standardize many planning processes in one large platform

Why it can be a harder fit here

  • It typically asks finance to learn and maintain a more specialized modeling environment instead of staying in Excel.
  • That usually means more dedicated admin ownership, more consulting support, and a slower ramp for the team.
  • For an FP&A-first rollout, that adds more change than is needed to fix reporting, forecast workflow, and response time.
Close and consolidation

OneStream

Strongest when close, consolidation, intercompany, and controller-led finance processes lead the project.

Where it is strong

  • Financial close and consolidation
  • Intercompany and elimination-heavy environments
  • Organizations leading with accounting control and consolidation first

Why it can be a harder fit here

  • It is a heavier platform to stand up and often brings more IT, admin, and consulting involvement.
  • FP&A can become secondary when the immediate need is management reporting, forecast support, and faster business analysis.
  • That makes it a stronger option for a controller-first transformation than for the first phase Veriforce needs now.
Web-based planning

Adaptive

Well known for budgeting and planning, especially in organizations already standardized on Workday.

Where it is strong

  • Core budgeting, forecasting, and operational planning
  • Organizations that prefer a web-based planning tool
  • Teams already centered around the Workday ecosystem

Why it can be a harder fit here

  • The user experience is still centered on a web-based application, which creates more adoption friction for an Excel-heavy finance team.
  • Many teams still end up back in Excel for flexibility, detailed analysis, and unique revenue or reporting needs.
  • That creates more risk of running two processes at once instead of truly improving ownership and consistency.
Recommended fit

Vena

Excel-native FP&A platform built to fit a Microsoft, NetSuite, and phased-rollout environment.

Why it stands out

  • Users work in a familiar Excel environment instead of learning an entirely new system.
  • That lowers adoption friction for finance and contributors, which matters when the team already lives in Excel, Teams, and Power BI.
  • It is built to work with the broader Microsoft ecosystem while also integrating cleanly with NetSuite and other source systems.
  • It is one unified platform for budgeting, forecasting, reporting, workflows, and analysis rather than a collection of separate tools.

What it would give Veriforce

  • One finance-owned model for actuals, management reporting, forecasts, budgets, and ad hoc analysis
  • Faster reporting refresh, stronger drilldown, and a more controlled budget and forecast process
  • Less dependence on spreadsheet workarounds, local files, and manual follow-up
  • A practical first phase for FP&A now, with room to expand into broader finance and accounting later
Bottom line

Vena is the strongest fit for what Veriforce needs to solve first.

The best answer here is not the heaviest platform. It is the platform that improves reporting, forecasting, and decision support now while fitting the way the team already works. Vena stands out because it keeps users in a familiar Excel environment, fits the Microsoft and NetSuite direction already underway, and gives finance a practical way to improve the process now without taking on unnecessary complexity in phase one.
