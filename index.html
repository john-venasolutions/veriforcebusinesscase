FP&A business case

Why this makes sense to address now

The current finance process is getting more structured, but budgeting, forecasting, management reporting, and leadership support still depend too heavily on large Excel models, manual refreshes, local inputs, and finance manually stitching the process together. As acquisitions, entity changes, and NetSuite rollout continue, the risk is not just inefficiency. It is slower answers, more room for human error, weaker accountability, and a process that is not scaling at the same pace as the business.

Recommended sequencing

  • Start with FP&A first: management reporting, forecast support, budget workflow, and ad hoc analysis.
  • Use that foundation to improve the process ahead of Budget 27.
  • Expand into broader accounting, close, and consolidation support after the core FP&A process is in place.

Problem statement

The immediate pain is FP&A.

NetSuite is becoming the actuals system and Fabric is becoming the data backbone, but management reporting, forecasting, budget inputs, and analysis still sit in large workbooks, manual mappings, custom formulas, and one-off logic. The result is a process that works, but takes too much effort to maintain, is harder to trust than it should be, and does not give leadership answers as quickly as the business needs.

Why now

The business is scaling faster than the process.

Budget 27 design work is starting now. Acquisitions continue to add entities, systems, and mapping complexity. The broader value-creation plan depends on stronger reporting, cleaner data, and more confidence in the numbers. A scalable FP&A foundation improves current operations and also supports valuation readiness, buyer diligence, and a cleaner long-term finance story.

1What is getting in the way today
This is not an Excel problem by itself. It is a process, data quality, and scale problem.

Current friction

  • Budgeting and forecasting are still handled in Excel while NetSuite is mostly actuals.
  • Management reporting still depends on large workbooks, manual refreshes, and custom logic.
  • Not every entity is fully standardized, so some subsidiaries still need manual inputs and local workarounds.
  • Intercompany activity, multiple entities, multiple currencies, and acquisitions add more moving parts every cycle.

What that causes

  • Too much time goes into building, fixing, and reconciling reports instead of analyzing what changed.
  • Ad hoc requests can still take days when leadership needs answers quickly.
  • Data quality depends too much on manual formulas, local mappings, and who last touched the file.
  • Finance is still handholding the process more than running a repeatable system with shared accountability.

Why leadership should care

  • The business is growing through acquisition and international expansion, so finance needs to scale with it.
  • Cleaner reporting, faster follow-up, and stronger data hygiene support better decisions on spend, hiring, and resource allocation.
  • Consistent reporting and cleaner diligence support a stronger valuation story over time.
  • The longer the current process remains in place, the more rework and inconsistency compounds.

How to read this

For illustration, assume revenue is roughly in the middle of a $100M to $300M range, or about $200M. A 10-basis-point improvement is 0.10% of revenue, which equals $200K. Fifteen basis points equals $300K. Twenty-five basis points equals $500K.

What can create the movement

The value comes from catching issues sooner and responding sooner on hiring pace, discretionary spend, billing and revenue assumptions, regional performance, acquisition integration, and resource allocation. Faster visibility changes the timing of the decision.

Why timing matters

If a forecast issue, margin problem, or reporting inconsistency is surfaced a month earlier, the business has more room to react. That can mean slowing spend sooner, adjusting headcount sooner, tightening assumptions sooner, or escalating a regional issue before it gets larger.

6How the main options compare
The best answer is not the platform with the longest feature list. It is the platform that best matches the immediate problem, the pace of change, and the way finance already works.

The immediate need is not a giant transformation across every finance process at once. The immediate need is a cleaner FP&A process: better management reporting, better forecast support, stronger workflow accountability, lower dependence on fragile spreadsheets, and a platform that can still grow into broader finance and accounting use cases later.

Enterprise planning

Anaplan

Known for large-scale planning across finance, supply chain, sales, and workforce.

Where it is strong

  • Cross-functional planning across multiple business areas
  • Large scenario modeling environments
  • Organizations standardizing many planning processes in one enterprise platform

What creates friction here

  • It typically asks finance to work in a more specialized modeling environment instead of staying in Excel.
  • That often means a steeper learning curve, more dedicated admin ownership, and more outside consulting support.
  • For an FP&A-first rollout, it can introduce more change, more cost, and more maintenance than this phase actually needs.
Close and consolidation

OneStream

Strongest when close, consolidation, intercompany, and controller-led finance processes are driving the project.

Where it is strong

  • Financial close and consolidation
  • Intercompany, eliminations, and accounting-heavy use cases
  • Organizations leading with controller and close priorities first

What creates friction here

  • It is a heavier platform to stand up and often brings more IT, admin, and consulting involvement.
  • FP&A can become secondary when the immediate need is reporting, forecast support, and business analysis.
  • That makes it a stronger fit when close and consolidation are the starting point, not when FP&A is the first problem to solve.
Web-based planning

Adaptive

Well known for budgeting and planning, especially in organizations already centered around Workday.

Where it is strong

  • Core budgeting, forecasting, and operational planning
  • Organizations that want a web-based planning experience
  • Teams already aligned to the Workday ecosystem

What creates friction here

  • The planning experience still lives in a web-based system, which can create more change-management friction for an Excel-heavy finance team.
  • Many teams still end up back in Excel for flexibility, detailed analysis, and unique reporting needs.
  • That creates the risk of running the system and spreadsheets in parallel instead of truly simplifying ownership and adoption.
Recommended fit

Vena

Excel-native FP&A platform built to fit a Microsoft, NetSuite, and phased-rollout environment.

Why it stands out

  • Users stay in a familiar Excel environment instead of having to learn an entirely new system.
  • That lowers adoption friction for finance and contributors, which matters in a team already working in Excel, Teams, and Power BI.
  • It fits the broader Microsoft direction already in place while also integrating with NetSuite, Fabric, Salesforce, and other source systems.
  • It is one unified platform for reporting, planning, workflows, and analysis rather than a collection of separate tools.

What it would do in practice

  • Create one finance-owned model for actuals, management reporting, forecasts, budgets, and ad hoc analysis
  • Improve refresh speed, drilldown, and confidence in the numbers
  • Reduce dependence on spreadsheet workarounds, local files, and manual follow-up
  • Provide a practical first phase for FP&A now, with room to expand into close, consolidation, and broader finance later
Bottom line

Vena is the strongest fit for what needs to be solved first.

The strongest answer is not the heaviest platform. It is the platform that improves reporting, forecasting, workflow accountability, and decision support now while fitting the way the team already works. Vena stands out because it keeps users in a familiar Excel environment, fits the Microsoft and NetSuite direction already underway, and gives finance a practical way to improve the process now without taking on unnecessary complexity in phase one.
